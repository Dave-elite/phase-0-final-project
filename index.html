CUPPING COURSE

Cupping is a sensory evaluation method used to assess the quality and characteristics of coffee.
In this process, freshly brewed coffee samples are systematically evaluated for aroma,
flavor, acidity, body, and aftertaste. It involves slurping the coffee to aerate it across
the palate, allowing for a thorough assessment of its sensory attributes. Cupping sessions
are crucial for coffee professionals to determine coffee quality, consistency, and potential
defects, thus informing decisions in sourcing, blending, and roasting.

COFFEE ROASTING COURSE

A course on coffee roasting covers the fundamental principles and techniques essential for
transforming raw coffee beans into flavorful roasted coffee. Students learn
about the chemical transformations during roasting, utilize various roasting
equipment to achieve specific roast profiles, and develop skills in quality
control through sensory evaluation and cupping. Emphasis is placed on safety, sustainability,
and staying current with industry trends and innovations, ensuring graduates are equipped
to excel in the dynamic field of coffee roasting.
